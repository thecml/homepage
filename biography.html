I am a PhD Student at the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, Aarhus University, Denmark. The focus of my PhD is survival analysis and time-to-event prediction for precision medicine. Previously, I received an M.Sc. in Computer Engineering and a B.Sc. in Software Engineering from Aarhus University.

Before joining the PhD programme, I worked as a Research Assistant in 2020 on a signature project by the Danish government called AIR. Our goal was to leverage machine learning to identify elderly individuals who were at risk of falling. The AIR project became a success and expanded into PRECISE in June 2022, funded by the European AAL programme. At the same time, I began pursuing a PhD in precision medicine at Aarhus University, focusing on survival analysis and time-to-event outcomes. As part of my PhD, I visited the Department of Computing Science at the University of Alberta, Canada, from March to June 2024, where I was supervised by Professor Russ Greiner.

I have also worked as a Student Software Engineer at Danske Bank developing enterprise solutions for B2B customers, and later as a Machine Learning Engineer at DigiRehab A/S.
