I am a PhD Student at the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, Aarhus University, Denmark. My research is about survival analysis and time-to-event prediction, with a particular focus on handling censoring and modeling multiple events. Previously, I received an M.Sc. in Computer Engineering and a B.Sc. in ICT Engineering from Aarhus University.

Before joining the PhD programme, I worked as a Research Assistant on a flagship project by the Danish government called AIR, in Danish that applied machine learning to prevent falls in community-dwelling elderly. In June 2022, the AIR project expanded into PRECISE funded by the European AAL programme. Since June 2022, I have been pursuing a PhD in precision medicine a Aarhus University, focusing on survival analysis and time-to-event prediction. As part of my PhD, I visited the Department of Computing Science at the University of Alberta, Canada, from March to June 2024, where I was supervised by Professor Russ Greiner.

I have also worked as a Student Software Engineer at Danske Bank developing enterprise solutions for B2B customers, and later as a Machine Learning Engineer at DigiRehab A/S.
